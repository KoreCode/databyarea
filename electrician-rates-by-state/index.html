Electrician Rates By State

Electrician costs aren’t universal — location, scope, and code requirements all influence the final number.

Many projects land near $90–$120/hr for labor, depending on local requirements.

What affects electrician pricing?

  • Licensing and continuing education requirements
  • Permit and inspection rules
  • Panel capacity and existing wiring condition
  • Access to walls, ceilings, and service panels

Common electrician projects

  • Outlet and switch replacements
  • Panel upgrades and breaker work
  • Lighting and fixture installation
  • Rewiring and circuit additions

How to compare quotes

  • Get at least two written estimates
  • Confirm the same scope is being priced
  • Ask about permits, inspections, and warranties
  • Clarify what causes additional charges

FAQ

Why does electrician pricing change by state?

Labor licensing, permit rules, inspection requirements, and local demand all affect final pricing.

Is hourly or flat-rate pricing better?

Hourly pricing works well for uncertain repairs, while flat-rate bids are usually better for clearly defined projects.

How can I avoid surprise charges?

Get the scope in writing, ask what triggers add-ons, and confirm permits and cleanup are included.
