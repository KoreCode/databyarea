Property Taxes in Minnesota

Minnesota property tax bills depend on more than home value. County, city, school district, classification, local levies, special assessments, and credits all shape the final annual bill. Use this guide to understand what to compare before buying, refinancing, or relocating.

How to Read a Minnesota Tax Bill

  • Estimated market value: The assessor's value estimate used in the tax calculation process.
  • Classification: Homestead, non-homestead, rental, commercial, agricultural, and other classes can be treated differently.
  • Taxing districts: County, city, township, school, special districts, and voter-approved levies may all appear.
  • Special assessments: Street, utility, drainage, or local improvement charges can change a parcel's total bill.

Buyer Checklist

  • Ask for the current tax statement and compare it with the listing's monthly payment estimate.
  • Confirm homestead status because the seller's bill may not match your future ownership use.
  • Check special assessments for street, sewer, water, sidewalk, or local improvement charges.
  • Review escrow assumptions with the lender if taxes are likely to change after purchase.
